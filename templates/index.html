Frequently Ask Questions

How do I sign up for online banking?

To sign up for online banking, visit our website and click on the "Sign Up" or "Register" button. Follow the instructions to create your online banking account.

Is online banking secure?

Yes, online banking is highly secure. We use advanced encryption and security protocols to protect your data. Additionally, we recommend strong password practices and provide account alerts for added security.

How do I transfer funds between accounts?

To transfer funds, log in to your online banking account, select the "Transfer Funds" option, choose the source and destination accounts, and follow the on-screen instructions to complete the transfer.

Can I set up recurring bill payments?

Yes, you can easily set up recurring bill payments. Navigate to the "Bill Pay" section, add your payees, and schedule recurring payments with specific frequencies and amounts.

What should I do if I forget my password?

If you forget your password, click on the "Forgot Password" link on the login page. Follow the prompts to reset your password. You may need to provide some account verification details.

How can I access e-statements?

To access your e-statements, log in to your online banking account and go to the "Account Statements" or "e-Statements" section. Select the account and statement period to view and download your statements.

Are there any fees for using online banking?

Online banking is generally free. However, some specific services or transactions may have associated fees. You can review our fee schedule on our website for more information.

How do I contact customer support for assistance?

You can reach our customer support team by phone or email. Visit the "Contact Us" section on our website to find our contact details, including phone numbers and email addresses for support. How to Keep Your Online Banking Secure?

Ham Brook • Jan 18, 2019• News

Ensuring the security of your online banking is paramount. Start by creating strong, unique passwords and enabling multi-factor authentication (MFA). Avoid public Wi-Fi networks for banking activities, keep your devices updated, and regularly review your account statements for any unauthorized transactions.

What's the Difference Between Savings and Fixed Deposit Accounts?

James Phelps • Jan 18, 2019• News

Savings and Fixed Deposit accounts serve different purposes. Savings accounts offer easy access to your money, ideal for everyday transactions. Fixed Deposit accounts, on the other hand, provide higher interest rates and are designed for long-term savings with restricted access.
